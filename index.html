Food Access Analysis: Camden County, NJ

By Vincent Lu & Ahmad Shahbaz | Rutgers 34:970:595: Command-Line GIS

Project Overview

This project explores the intersection of socioeconomic status and food access in Camden County, NJ, identifying areas where low income and low physical access to groceries overlap.

Data Methodology

Sources & Preparation: Data was sourced from the USDA Food Access Research Atlas (2019), the US Census Bureau (ACS 5-Year Estimates, 2015-2019), and NJ Transit Open Data[cite: 15, 16]. Census data was accessed via the tidycensus and cenpy libraries.

Processing: The original data was in GeoJSON and CSV formats[cite: 17]. To make the data mappable, we performed a spatial join between NJ Transit bus stop point data and Census Tract polygons to calculate transit density[cite: 17, 24]. We also aggregated retail points from OpenStreetMap using osmnx[cite: 17].

Quality & Reliability: We addressed data quality by filtering for Coefficient of Variation (CV). Any Census variables with a $CV \ge 40$ were symbolized as "unreliable" or excluded to ensure statistical integrity[cite: 18, 21].
