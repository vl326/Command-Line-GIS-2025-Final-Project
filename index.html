Food Deserts in New Jersey

Mapping Access to Healthy Food in Camden County
Vincent Lu and Ahmad Shahbaz | 34:970:595 Command-Line GIS | Fall 2025

Research Questions

  1. Where are the food deserts in New Jersey, and how do their characteristics differ across communities?
  2. What factors are most correlated with food desert locations?

About the Data

USDA Food Access Research Atlas - tract-level measures of food access including Low Income and Low Access (LILA) indicators that define food deserts. updated 2021 using 2019 data.

American Community Survey (ACS) 5-Year Estimates (2019) - demographic and economic data including median household income, race/ethnicity. we calculated Coefficient of Variation (CV) to identify unreliable estimates (CV >= 40%).

Census Bureau TIGER/Line Shapefiles - 2019 cartographic boundary files for nj census tracts.

Data Processing: table join connecting usda food access data to tract geometries using 11-digit fips codes. created centroid points from food desert polygons for interactive mapping.

Study Area: camden county has 14 food deserts out of 127 tracts (11%) and the most vehicle-related food deserts in new jersey.

Conclusions

  • food deserts are concentrated in areas with higher poverty rates and larger black populations - environmental justice concern
  • many food desert tracts also have low vehicle access - compounding difficulty reaching supermarkets
  • vulnerable populations (seniors, children) disproportionately affected by limited food access

addressing food insecurity in camden county requires solutions considering both spatial distribution of food retailers and transportation accessibility
